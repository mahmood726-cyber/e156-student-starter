The rewrite-workbook has a strict protection contract that AI tools MUST respect:

1. **CURRENT BODY** — the AI-generated version. Update freely UNLESS
   `SUBMITTED: [x]` is set (then frozen).
2. **YOUR REWRITE** — YOUR rewrite. The AI **NEVER** touches this, even if
   it violates E156 rules. This is your voice; the AI's job is to suggest,
   not to edit.
3. **SUBMITTED** — `[ ]` or `[x]`. Only you toggle this.

**Why:** The workbook is a submission history record. AI editing YOUR REWRITE
destroys the paper trail of your own revisions. Past incidents have had AI
tools silently "improve" a student's rewrite, then the student couldn't tell
which words were theirs vs the AI's — which makes the authorship declaration
on submission false.

**How to apply:** If you ask the AI to revise your paper, paste ONLY the
CURRENT BODY (the AI-generated text) and tell it to produce a new CURRENT
BODY. Never paste YOUR REWRITE and ask for edits. If an AI tool offers to
"improve both versions" or "merge them", say no — that violates the contract.
The only time YOUR REWRITE changes is when YOU change it, by hand.
